| 8:30 | HR & Admin | 30 min | Logistics, policies, contacts |

### What to Bring
- Notebook (take notes — shows you care)
- Pen
- Phone (but put away during 1:1s)
- Open mind
- Curiosity

### What to Say in All-Hands
"I'm excited to be here. I come from [background], and I'm committed to three things: technical excellence, great people, and delivering results. This week I'm learning. I'll have 1:1s with all of you, I'll listen more than I talk, and I'd love your input."

### Red Flags to Notice
- Are people hesitant to speak up? (may indicate trust issues)
- Do managers blame their teams? (toxic culture signal)
- Is there evidence of decisions being made slowly? (governance issues)
- Are people checking out at 5 PM? (morale / engagement issue)

### People You MUST Meet by EOD Friday
- All direct reports (engineering managers)
- Head of Product
- Your manager
- 3-5 individual engineers (not through managers)
- Security/Compliance lead

## Key Questions Template (For Any 1:1)

### Standard 1:1 Structure (30 min)
1. **Personal check-in (2 min):** "How are you doing?"
2. **Role overview (8 min):** "Tell me about your team/role"
3. **Deep dive (12 min):** "What's working? What's frustrating?"
4. **Growth (5 min):** "Where do you want to grow?"
5. **Logistics (3 min):** "How do you like to work?"

### Questions That Work Everywhere
- "What are you most proud of in the last 6 months?"
- "What's keeping you up at night right now?"
- "What do you need from me to be successful?"
- "If you could change one thing about how we work, what would it be?"
- "What does the team do well?"
- "Where do you want to be in 2 years?"

### How to Listen
✅ Take notes (show you care)  
✅ Pause before responding (think, don't react)  
✅ Use "tell me more" (go deeper)  
✅ Refer back to what they said (show you were listening)  
✅ Say thank you (genuinely)  

❌ Don't interrupt  
❌ Don't make promises yet  
❌ Don't criticize previous leadership  
❌ Don't make announcements

## Red Flags & How to Handle Them

| Red Flag | What It Means | What to Do |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| Multiple people mention same pain point | Consensus — this is real | Don't fix immediately, understand root cause first |
| Hesitant answers in 1:1s | Trust issues or fear | Keep listening, show confidentiality, be consistent |
| Manager blames team | Potential toxic culture | Observe more, watch if this pattern repeats |
| No evidence of technical debt management | Quality will degrade | This becomes a priority |
| Slow deployment cycle | Delivery risk | Understand why (process? tools? skills?) |
| High attrition or interview culture | People are checking out | Needs attention but not day 1 |
| Siloed teams | Communication breakdown | Watch how org handles cross-team work |
| No clear decision-making process | Risk of chaos | Document how decisions actually get made |

## What NOT to Do This Week

❌ **Don't announce changes** — You don't understand the context yet  
❌ **Don't criticize the previous leader** — You'll lose trust  
❌ **Don't make promises** — You might not be able to keep them  
❌ **Don't pick sides** — In any conflict, stay neutral (for now)  
❌ **Don't spend all day in email** — People first, admin later  
❌ **Don't skip 1:1s** — This is how you build relationships  
❌ **Don't go silent** — Visibility matters, let people see you working

## Credibility Signals This Week

**Build trust by:**
- ✅ Listening more than talking
- ✅ Asking thoughtful questions
- ✅ Taking notes and remembering details
- ✅ Saying "I don't know" when you don't
- ✅ Following up ("I said I'd find out — here's what I learned")
- ✅ Respecting people's time (no rambling, stick to schedule)
- ✅ Being warm (smile, use names, say thank you)
- ✅ Showing respect for what they've built

**Avoid undermining trust by:**
- ❌ Pretending to know things you don't
- ❌ Making quick judgments
- ❌ Telling people how things will change
- ❌ Gossiping or repeating confidences
- ❌ Being distant or cold
- ❌ Showing up late or unprepared

## Weekly Rhythm (What to Repeat Each Week for First Month)

### Every Monday
- Team all-hands or update
- Assess week's progress on priorities
- Prep for 1:1s
- Scan Jira for blockers

### Every Wednesday
- Mid-week pulse check (how's the week going?)
- Any critical issues to address?
- Cross-functional sync if possible

### Every Friday
- 1:1 with your manager (what did you learn?)
- Retrospective (what went well? what didn't?)
- Plan next week
- Celebrate any wins

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Better Approach |
|---------|------------|-----------------|
| Trying to learn everything in one week | Overwhelm, miss nuance | Spread over 2-3 weeks, iterate |
| Asking only your managers' perspective | One-sided view | Talk to ICs and cross-functions |
| Making quick judgments | Wrong conclusions, lost trust | Listen for patterns, revisit later |
| Spending time in email | Miss relationship building | Email is low priority week 1 |
| Announcing vision early | People tune out, resist change | Build consensus first, vision later |
| Skipping security briefing | Miss critical context | Treat this as mandatory, dig deep |
| Not documenting anything | Forget details later | Write down everything |
| Picking a favorite team/person early | Risks favoritism | Be equally curious about everyone |

## When You Feel Stuck or Overwhelmed

**Remember:**
- You have the hardest job in the first week: you're learning and building relationships simultaneously
- It's OK to not have all answers yet
- People respect curiosity and humility more than false confidence
- Document everything — you'll need it to measure progress in month 2
- Your job is to listen, not to fix everything immediately

**If you're unsure, ask yourself:**
1. "Am I listening more than talking?" (Should be 70/30)
2. "Have I talked to enough people?" (Should be 10+ by Thursday)
3. "Do I understand the pain points?" (Should be clear by Thursday)
4. "Do I have quick wins identified?" (Should have 1-2 by Thursday)

If yes to all 4, you're on track.
