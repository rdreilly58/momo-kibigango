## Overview

Your first day is about **establishing presence, building trust, and observing without committing**. You are in "learning mode" — information gathering, relationship initiation, and credibility signaling through curiosity and respect for what came before.

**Key Principle (Watkins):** "Don't assume you know what to do. Observe, ask questions, and gather information before implementing changes."

**Primary Goals:**
1. ✅ Meet your direct reports and key stakeholders (relationships)
2. ✅ Understand organizational structure, team dynamics, and current projects
3. ✅ Set tone: collaborative, curious, committed to excellence
4. ✅ Gather baseline intelligence on pain points and strengths
5. ✅ Establish working norms (1:1 cadence, office hours, communication)

---

## 8:00 AM – Arrival & Preparation (30 min)

**What to Do:**
- Arrive early to settle in, review office location and building logistics
- Locate: restrooms, cafeteria, parking, badge access, IT support contact
- Test email access, calendar, Slack/Teams, and Jira access
- Take a walk around the office — observe how teams are arranged, energy level, who's in early

**What to Observe:**
- Is the office space open or compartmented? (tells you about security/classification levels)
- How are teams physically arranged? (do managers sit with their teams? are there silos?)
- What's on the walls? (whiteboards with architecture, sprint boards, posters, awards?)
- What's the vibe? (hustle, calm, stressed, collaborative?)
- Who's early? (early risers often indicate high engagement or time zone mismatches for remote)

**Credibility Signals:**
- Be organized and punctual — shows you respect others' time
- Smile and greet people you pass — warmth matters on day one
- Don't look lost — you're curious, not confused

---

## 8:30 AM – Welcome from HR & Admin (30 min)

**What to Do:**
- Meet with HR for 20 minutes (benefits, payroll, process overview, building access)
- Meet with Admin/Office Manager for 10 minutes (calendar management, scheduling preferences)

**Key Questions to Ask HR:**
- "What are the top 3 things I need to know about Leidos' culture?"
- "How are career development and promotions handled here?"
- "What's the process for new hires / people decisions / managing performance issues?"
- "Are there any organizational changes happening that I should be aware of?"

**What to Document:**
- HR contact name and phone number
- Admin contact for scheduling
- Key policy documents (vacation, security, compliance)

---

## 9:00 AM – Executive Alignment (60 min)

**Who:** Your direct manager / leadership sponsor (likely VP or Director level)

**What to Do:**
- Listen primarily — this is their chance to set context
- Take notes on their priorities, expectations, and success criteria for your first 90 days
- Ask clarifying questions, not challenging questions

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- "What are the biggest challenges you see in engineering right now?"
- "What does success look like for me in the first 30/60/90 days?"
- "Who are the key stakeholders I should know? Any relationships I need to build early?"
- "Are there any organizational or political dynamics I should be aware of?"
- "What were the pain points with the previous leadership structure?"
- "What's your communication preference? How often should we sync?"

**What to Observe:**
- What gets animated? (tells you what they care about)
- What concerns do they mention? (technical debt? delivery? people?)
- How do they describe the team? (proud? frustrated? optimistic?)
- What's unsaid? (if they don't mention AI, delivery, or culture — it might not be top priority)

**Credibility Signals:**
- Take detailed notes (shows you're serious)
- Ask thoughtful questions, not obvious ones
- Acknowledge what worked before ("I want to build on what's been good here")
- No promises yet ("I'll have a better sense after I meet the teams")

---

## 10:00 AM – Individual Conversations with Direct Reports (Engineering Managers / Senior Leaders)

**Format:** 30 minutes each with first batch of direct reports
- Stagger these across the first week if there are many (see first week plan)
- For today, aim for 2-3 key direct reports (engineering managers, principal engineers, or team leads reporting to you)

**Setup:**
- Private, quiet room
- Calendar invite sent in advance (if time allows)
- Phone/laptop put away (no distractions)

**What to Do:**
- Start with a personal check-in ("How's your week been?")
- Spend 70% listening, 30% sharing
- Ask open-ended questions
- Take notes

**Key Questions to Ask Each Direct Report:**
1. "Tell me about your team — what do you do? Who are the key people?"
2. "What are you most proud of in the last 6 months?"
3. "What's keeping you up at night? What's your biggest challenge right now?"
4. "What do you need from leadership to be successful?"
5. "If you could change one thing about how things work, what would it be?"
6. "How do you prefer to work? (remote? in-office? communication style?)"
7. "What are your growth goals? Where do you want to be in 2 years?"

**What to Observe:**
- Energy and passion — are they engaged or cynical?
- Candor — do they feel safe being honest with you?
- Technical vs. people focus — what occupies their mind?
- Team health signals — do they mention retention issues, conflicts, morale?
- Clarity — do they understand strategy, priorities, expectations?

**Credibility Signals:**
- Use their name
- Refer back to what they said ("You mentioned X — tell me more about that")
- Write things down
- Say "thank you" and mean it
- No judgment of previous decisions
- No promises ("I'll look into that and we can discuss next week")

**Script:**
"Hi [Name], thanks for making time for this. I'm brand new, so I'm trying to understand the landscape, the people, and how we can work well together. I'd love to hear about your team, what you're working on, and what success looks like for you. I'll mostly listen today."

## 11:30 AM – Walk Through Office / Project Spaces (30 min)

**What to Do:**
- Walk the office with one of your direct reports or your manager
- Ask to see the main development areas, sprint boards, collaboration spaces
- Stop and chat with engineers at their desks (informal, 2-3 minute chats)
- Look at whiteboards, project displays, and team setup

**What to Observe:**
- Physical organization — is it chaos or order?
- Collaboration signals — do people talk across desks? headphones on? isolated?
- Project visibility — can you see what teams are working on from the boards?
- Security posture — any classified material visibility? any CUI markings? secure spaces?
- Team energy — are people focused, chatting, stressed?

**Credibility Signals:**
- Greet people by name if possible (show you care about individuals)
- Ask genuine questions about their work ("What are you building?")
- Don't deep-dive into technical issues — that's not day one
- Notice and comment on things you see ("Looks like you're in the middle of a big sprint")

---

## 12:00 PM – Lunch (60 min)

**Who:** Invite one or two of your direct reports or key peers
- Make it casual
- Let them choose the location if possible
- Expense it yourself on day one (sets a tone of generosity and respect)

**What to Do:**
- Continue the listening mode
- Ask about non-work things
- Let them set the conversation

**What NOT to Do:**
- Don't make big announcements about changes
- Don't criticize previous leadership
- Don't commit to major decisions

---

## 1:00 PM – Dive into Systems & Dashboards (90 min)

**What to Do:**
- Sit with engineering lead or tech lead to tour:
  - Jira (current sprints, team velocity, backlog)
  - CI/CD pipeline (deployment frequency, recent failures, build times)
  - Monitoring / observability dashboard (error rates, latency, availability)
  - Architecture documentation (if it exists — likely in Confluence or similar)
  - GitHub repos (project structure, recent commits, PR activity)

**Key Questions to Ask:**
- "What are we currently working on? What's the top priority?"
- "What's our deployment frequency? How long does a typical deployment take?"
- "What are the biggest technical challenges right now?"
- "What tools and practices are we using for testing, security scanning, monitoring?"
- "How do we handle technical debt? Is there a backlog for it?"
- "What's the state of CI/CD — are teams able to deploy frequently and safely?"

**What to Observe:**
- Jira discipline — are stories well-written? is the board up-to-date? velocity trend?
- Deployment frequency — daily? weekly? monthly? (tells you about delivery maturity)
- Build times — are they reasonable or are teams waiting for CI?
- Error patterns — are the same issues recurring? signs of larger problems?
- Documentation — is the architecture clear or tribal knowledge?
- Security posture — are SAST/dependency scanning in place?

**Credibility Signals:**
- Ask about pain points ("What takes the longest in your process?")
- Acknowledge good practices ("Your test coverage looks solid")
- Don't pretend to understand everything ("I'll need to dig deeper into that")
- Suggest nothing yet — just learn

---

## 2:30 PM – Coffee / Informal Conversations (30 min)

**What to Do:**
- Walk around with coffee
- Stop by 3-4 individual engineers' desks (not managers, engineers)
- 5 minutes each — super informal

**Key Questions:**
- "What are you working on?"
- "How long have you been at Leidos?"
- "What do you enjoy most about working here?"

**What to Observe:**
- Candor — do they speak freely or carefully?
- Pride in work — do they care about what they're building?
- Retention signals — are they thinking about leaving? is it a good team?

---

## 3:00 PM – Security & Compliance Briefing (45 min)

**Important for Leidos context:** Defense sector = security first

**Who:** Security officer or compliance lead

**What to Learn:**
- Classification levels (Unclassified, CUI, SECRET, TOP SECRET)
- How information is marked and handled
- Secure communication requirements (email? Slack? encrypted channels?)
- Access controls — who can see what?
- Incident reporting procedures
- Any current security concerns or initiatives
- Defense Contractor requirements (DFARS, NIST, etc.)

**Key Question:**
- "What are the top 3 security risks we're managing right now?"

**Credibility Signals:**
- Take this seriously — security = mission critical in defense
- Ask about how AI tools are handled (ChatGPT, Copilot, etc. — likely restricted)
- Show respect for the process

---

## 3:45 PM – Final 1:1 with Direct Report / Peer (30 min)

**Who:** Another key direct report you didn't meet yet, or a peer at your level in another function (Product, Operations, etc.)

**Goal:** Diversify your perspective — understand how other functions see engineering

**Key Question:**
- "What does the Product / Operations / Design team need from engineering to be successful?"
