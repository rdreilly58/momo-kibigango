I've been thinking about this for months, and today I'm announcing momo-kiji — an attempt to answer one of the biggest questions in Apple computing:

**"Why is there no CUDA for Apple Neural Engine?"**

Here's the story:

NVIDIA didn't dominate AI because they made good GPUs. They dominated because they built CUDA — a clear, documented, powerful SDK that let developers use GPU hardware effectively.

Apple has ANE in every device since M1. It's in your Mac, your iPhone, your iPad. It's 10-100x more efficient than GPUs for inference.

But there's no SDK.

Developers who want to use ANE either:
1. Use CoreML (limited control, black box performance)
2. Reverse-engineer private APIs (risky, undocumented)
3. Hope their models just work (they don't)

This is a massive opportunity being left on the table.

Two weeks ago, researchers published the Orion paper (ArXiv 2603.06728), detailing ANE internals in unprecedented detail. The community is ready. The research exists. The need is obvious.

So we're building momo-kiji: CUDA for Apple Neural Engine.

**What momo-kiji is:**
→ Open-source framework for ANE development
→ Clear API (like CUDA, but simpler)
→ Open compiler architecture
→ Debugging & profiling tools
→ Community standards

**What momo-kiji isn't:**
→ A replacement for CoreML (complements it)
→ Affiliated with Apple (independent, open)

**Why I'm excited:**

This is a rare moment where research, community interest, hardware timing, and opportunity all align. In 5 years, I want developers writing ANE kernels like they write CUDA kernels — with clarity, confidence, and access to great tools.
