**Primary Goals:**
1. ✅ Meet all direct reports (1:1s) and key stakeholders
2. ✅ Complete listening tour (product, operations, security, quality, customer success)
3. ✅ Understand technical baseline (DORA metrics, architecture, testing, CI/CD state)
4. ✅ Identify pain points (consensus themes from multiple people)
5. ✅ Identify 1-2 quick wins (things you can move on in week 2-3 to build momentum)
6. ✅ Establish working norms and cadence for the following weeks
7. ✅ Begin forming a point of view (observations, not yet decisions)

**Key Principle:** "Listen to everyone, commit to nothing." — Watkins

## MONDAY, March 23 (FIRST DAY)

### 9:00 AM – Team All-Hands Meeting (60 min)

**What to Do:**
- Present yourself briefly (5 minutes max)
- Welcome people, express excitement about role
- Set expectations for the week (listening, learning, 1:1s)
- Ask for their help ("I need your input to understand how we can be better")

**Talking Points (40 words or less):**
"I'm excited to be here. I come from [background], and I'm committed to three things: technical excellence, great people, and delivering results. This week I'm learning. I'll have 1:1s with all of you, I'll listen more than I talk, and I'd love your input on what's working and what's not. Let's build great things together."

**What NOT to Do:**
- Don't announce any changes or decisions
- Don't criticize previous leadership
- Don't make promises you're not sure you can keep
- Don't get deep into technical details

**Credibility Signals:**
- Smile and show genuine warmth
- Make eye contact (if team is in-person)
- Keep it short — respect people's time
- Invite questions ("What do you want me to know?")

### 10:00 AM – Engineering Managers 1:1s (3 back-to-back, 30 min each)

**Who:** Your direct reports (likely 2-4 engineering managers)

**Format:**
- Private room
- 30 minutes each
- Calendar invite already sent (from day 1 planning)

**Structure (30 min total):**
- Personal check-in (2 min): "How are you doing?"
- Team overview (8 min): "Tell me about your team, what you're building, current challenges"
- Deep dive (12 min): "What's working well? What's your biggest frustration right now?"
- Growth (5 min): "What do you want to get better at?"
- Logistics (3 min): "How do you like to communicate? Any conflicts I should know about?"

**Key Questions:**
1. "Who are your strongest performers? Anyone you're worried about?"
2. "What would make your job easier?"
3. "If you could change one thing about how we operate, what would it be?"
4. "How's the relationship with Product/Design/Operations? Any friction?"
5. "Are you thinking about staying, growing here, or exploring options?"
6. "What do you wish the previous leadership had done differently?"
7. "What's the biggest risk to our delivery right now?"

**What to Observe:**
- Passion — do they care about the work and their people?
- Candor — do they feel safe with you yet? (probably not fully yet)
- Pain points — what comes up naturally? (that's what matters)
- Conflict signals — any tension with peers or product?
- Retention risk — are they engaged or checking out?

**How to Listen Effectively (Best Practices 2025-2026):**
- Pause before responding (shows you're thinking, not reacting)
- Use "tell me more" instead of jumping in
- Refer back to previous things they said ("Earlier you mentioned X...")
- Take notes (shows you value their input)
- No judgment — if they complain, say "thanks for being honest"

**Script:**
"Thanks for spending time with me. I want to understand your world — your team, your challenges, what you need from me to be successful. I'll listen more than I talk today. Sound good?"

**By End of Monday:**
- You'll have a clear picture of the people and project landscape
- You'll know who the strong performers are
- You'll hear pain points from three perspectives

### 12:30 PM – Lunch with Product Lead (60 min)

**Who:** Head of Product or equivalent

**Why:** Product alignment is critical to delivery success

**Key Questions:**
- "How's the relationship between engineering and product right now?"
- "What's our roadmap for the next 2-3 quarters?"
- "What's the biggest challenge you see in delivery?"
- "What do you need from engineering to move faster?"
- "How do you handle trade-offs between feature development and technical debt?"

**What to Observe:**
- Does product feel heard by engineering?
- Is there mutual respect or blame?
- How are priorities set?

### 2:00 PM – Jira / Metrics Deep Dive (90 min)

**Who:** Tech lead, engineering manager, or metrics/data analyst

**What to Do:**
- Review last 2-3 sprints in detail
- Understand current Jira setup, workflows, definition of done
- Extract DORA metrics baseline:
  - Deployment frequency (how often do we ship?)
  - Lead time for changes (idea to production)
  - Change failure rate (rollbacks, hotfixes, failed deployments)
  - Mean time to recovery (how fast to fix issues?)

**Questions:**
- "What's our current deployment frequency?"
- "How long does it take from PR to production?"
- "What's the failure rate? What typically fails?"
- "How do we prioritize between new features, testing, technical debt?"
- "What's blocking faster deployment?"

**What to Document:**
- Current Jira workflow (screenshot or document)
- DORA metrics baseline (even rough estimates)
- Bottlenecks in the process (where does work get stuck?)
- Quality metrics (test coverage %, bug escape rate, etc.)

**Credibility Signals:**
- Ask about what's working ("Your test coverage looks solid")
- Acknowledge complexity ("This is a lot to manage")
- No judgment of current state

### 3:30 PM – Individual Engineer Chats (4 x 15 min each)

**Who:** Senior engineers or individual contributors (not managers)

**Why:** Hear from people doing the work day-to-day

**Format:**
- Informal (coffee, hallway, their desk)
- 15 minutes max
- Focus on one thing: "What do you wish you could change about how we work?"

**Key Question:**
- "What's one thing that would make your job easier or more enjoyable?"

**Listen for:**
- Process friction ("Too many meetings", "reviews take forever")
- Technical debt ("We want to refactor X but never have time")
- Tools/environment ("Our tooling is slow", "CI takes 30 min")
- People issues ("My manager doesn't understand my growth goals")

### 5:00 PM – Reflection & Update Notes

**What to Do:**
- Update your "First Day Observations" document with Monday's learnings
- Look for patterns:
  - What pain point came up 2+ times?
  - What quick win is mentioned?
  - Who are the key influencers (people others respect)?
  - What's the team's biggest strength?

### 1:00 PM – Security & Compliance Deep Dive (2 hours)

**Who:** Security officer, compliance lead, or CIO

**Why:** Critical for defense sector context. You need to understand:
- Data classification (Unclassified, CUI, SECRET, etc.)
- Compliance requirements (DFARS, NIST, etc.)
- Security tools and practices (SAST, scanning, incident response)
- Current security initiatives or gaps
- How AI tools are governed

**Key Questions:**
- "What are the top 3 security risks in engineering right now?"
- "How do we handle classified information? What's the process?"
- "What's our posture on generative AI tools (ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot)? Are they restricted?"
- "What security scanning is in place in CI/CD?"
- "What's the incident response process if we have a breach?"
- "Are there any upcoming security audits or compliance events I should know about?"

**What to Learn:**
- Security is part of your job — not separate from engineering
- Defense sector expects security-first thinking
- You need to be able to explain to teams why security matters

**Credibility Signals:**
- Take this seriously (it directly impacts national security)
- Ask smart questions (show you're not naive about security)
- Commit to learning the compliance landscape

### 3:00 PM – Architecture Review with Principal Engineer / Architect (90 min)

**Who:** Your principal engineer or most senior technical person

**Why:** Understand the current technical foundation — this is critical context for your technical leadership role

**What to Learn:**
- System architecture (diagrams, design patterns, key technologies)
- Technology stack (Java, Rust, JavaScript, React — how are they used?)
- Major architectural decisions and why they were made
- Known technical debt (what are we managing?)
- Testing and QA practices (unit tests, integration tests, E2E?)
- Deployment architecture (how does code get from laptop to production?)

**Key Questions:**
- "Walk me through our architecture at a high level"
- "What are the biggest technical challenges we're solving?"
- "What would you redesign if you could start over?"
- "What's the quality of the codebase? Any areas of concern?"
- "How automated is testing? What's our test coverage?"
- "What's on your wish list for technical improvements?"

**What to Document:**
- System architecture diagrams (ask for them)
- Technology decisions (why Java vs. Rust? why React?)
- Known technical debt areas
- Testing coverage baseline

## Key Principles to Remember All Week

**Listen More Than You Talk**
- Your job is to understand, not to teach
- 70% listening, 30% talking

**No Announcements Yet**
- This week is observation
- Next week you can start suggesting improvements

**Document Everything**
- Good memory is great, but written notes are better
- You'll need this baseline in month 2 to measure progress

**Be Curious, Not Judgmental**
- If something seems broken, ask "why" before assuming it is
- There's usually a good reason for how things are

**Build Relationships First**
- People work for people they trust and respect
- Earning trust now pays dividends for months
